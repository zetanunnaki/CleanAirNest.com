## What You Get for Under $90

The Levoit LV-H132 is a small, cylindrical air purifier built for bedrooms, offices, and other compact spaces up to 129 sq. ft. It uses a three-stage filtration system — pre-filter, True HEPA filter, and activated carbon filter — to capture dust, pollen, smoke, and household odors. At under $90, it is one of the most affordable True HEPA purifiers available.

This is not a replacement for the Blueair Blue Pure 211+ in terms of capability. The 211+ was a $300 large-room workhorse covering 540 sq. ft. The LV-H132 covers roughly a quarter of that area. They serve entirely different needs.

## Filtration and Performance

The three-stage system handles the basics well. The pre-filter catches hair and large dust particles. The True HEPA filter captures 99.97% of airborne particles down to 0.3 microns, including pollen, mold spores, and fine dust. The activated carbon layer helps with light odors from cooking or pets.

Based on customer reviews, the LV-H132 performs reliably in small bedrooms and home offices. Multiple reviewers report noticeable improvements in dust levels and morning allergy symptoms within a few days of use. Keep in mind the 129 sq. ft. coverage — this purifier will struggle in any room larger than a standard bedroom.

## Noise and Night Light

Noise is where the LV-H132 earns praise. On its lowest fan speed, it measures around 25 dB — quieter than a whisper. Even on the highest setting, it stays at 50 dB, roughly the level of a quiet conversation. For bedroom use, the low setting provides effective filtration without disturbing sleep.

The built-in night light is a small but appreciated touch. It offers a soft glow that works well as an ambient light in bedrooms or nurseries. You can turn it off independently of the fan if you prefer total darkness.

## Running Costs

The LV-H132 uses replacement filters (model B07BF91M9L) that typically run $15-20 each and need replacing every 6-8 months depending on usage. With energy costs factored in, expect to spend roughly $30-45 per year to operate this purifier. That is among the lowest running costs of any True HEPA purifier.

## What It Lacks

The LV-H132 is a manual, no-frills machine. There is no app control, no Wi-Fi connectivity, no air quality sensor, and no auto mode. You get three fan speeds and a power button. If you want smart features or automatic adjustments, look at the Levoit Core 300 or Core 400S instead.

The small coverage area is the biggest limitation. At 129 sq. ft., this purifier is genuinely only effective in small rooms. Living rooms, open floor plans, and studios are beyond its reach.
