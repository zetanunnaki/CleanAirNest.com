## Hisense 50-Pint with Built-In Pump: What You Get

The Hisense 50-Pint replaces the discontinued 70-pint model as Hisense's go-to residential dehumidifier. ## Design & Portability

The Hisense 50-Pint features a black finish that blends into basements, garages, and utility rooms better than the white housing on most dehumidifiers. It includes caster wheels and a carry handle for repositioning between rooms.

The built-in pump is the standout feature. Instead of relying on gravity drainage or manually emptying a bucket, you can route the pump hose to a sink, floor drain, or even upward to a window. This makes it practical for basements where the nearest drain is above the unit. A standard bucket is also included for situations where pump drainage is not set up.

## Performance

With 50 pints of daily moisture removal, this unit handles spaces up to 4,500 sq. ft. according to Hisense's specifications. Based on customer feedback, it performs well in basements, bathrooms, and garages with moderate to high humidity. The 48 dB noise level is quieter than the old 70-pint model (which ran at 52 dB), making it more livable in finished basements.

The unit is Energy Star certified, which means lower electricity bills compared to non-certified models running similar capacity. For a dehumidifier that may run 12-18 hours per day during humid months, this adds up.
