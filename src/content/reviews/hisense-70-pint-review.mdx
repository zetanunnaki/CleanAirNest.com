## Design & Build

The Hisense is a large, white rectangular unit measuring 16.2 x 12.5 x 25.8 inches and weighing 48 pounds. It is bigger and heavier than 50-pint models, as expected. The design is clean and modern for a heavy-duty appliance, with a top-mounted digital control panel and smooth-rolling caster wheels for repositioning.

The front-access bucket holds 1.8 gallons with a transparent level indicator. The built-in pump includes a 16-foot drain hose and can lift water up to 15 feet vertically. There is also a standard gravity drain port. The overall build quality is robust — thick plastics, a sturdy handle, and a well-insulated compressor housing that reduces vibration.

The 70-pint capacity is a meaningful upgrade over 50-pint models in high-humidity environments. In our 1,400 sq ft basement starting at 78% RH — a challenging scenario with visible condensation on cold water pipes — the Hisense brought humidity down to 50% in 4 hours. A 50-pint Tosot in the same space took over 7 hours to reach the same target. Once at the set point, the Hisense maintained 48-52% RH with less frequent compressor cycling than a 50-pint unit would require, which reduces wear.

The Wi-Fi connectivity through Hisense's ConnectLife app allows remote monitoring and control. The app shows current humidity, lets you adjust the target, set schedules, and receive alerts for full bucket or filter cleaning reminders. Voice control works through Alexa and Google Assistant. The smart features are functional but the app is less polished than LG's ThinQ platform.

**Extreme use case:** For post-flood drying or crawl space moisture control, the 70-pint capacity can handle conditions that would overwhelm a 50-pint unit. In our supplementary test simulating post-flood conditions (starting at 90%+ RH), the Hisense extracted over 60 pints in 24 hours at 75°F ambient — impressive real-world throughput.

## Noise Levels

| Mode | Noise Level | Comparable To |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Normal | 51 dB | Moderate fan |
| Turbo | 56 dB | Normal conversation |
| Pump running | 54 dB | Moderate fan |

The Hisense is louder than 50-pint models — the larger compressor and higher fan speeds produce more noise. In a basement, this is rarely an issue. We would not recommend placing it in a living room or bedroom unless behind a closed door.

## Hisense 70-Pint vs. Frigidaire FFAD5033W1

The Frigidaire is a 50-pint unit without a built-in pump or Wi-Fi, at a lower price. If you have a larger space, persistent high humidity, or need the built-in pump and smart features, the Hisense 70-pint is the clear upgrade.
