## What Changed From the 5500-2 to the 5510

| Feature | Winix 5500-2 | Winix 5510 |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| CADR | 232 CFM | 253 CFM |
| Coverage | 360 sq. ft. | 392 sq. ft. |
| WiFi / App | No | Yes |
| Air Quality Monitor | No | Yes — real-time display |
| Noise (Low) | 27.8 dB | 23.5 dB |
| Noise (Max) | 55.3 dB | 65.7 dB |
| Price | ~$160 | $179.99 |

The 5510 is quieter on low, more powerful at max, and adds the smart features the 5500-2 always lacked. The trade-off is a louder max speed — 65.7 dB vs. 55.3 dB — but auto mode rarely pushes to max unless air quality drops sharply.

## Performance

The 253 CFM CADR is a meaningful upgrade. In a 400 sq. ft. room, the 5510 cycles the air roughly once every 6 minutes — fast enough to handle cooking smoke, pet dander bursts, or a freshly vacuumed room within minutes.

The built-in air quality sensor feeds auto mode, which adjusts fan speed based on real-time particulate readings. This is the single biggest improvement over the 5500-2, which had auto mode but no visible air quality feedback.

## The PlasmaWave Feature

The 5510 carries over PlasmaWave technology — a bipolar ionization feature that creates hydroxyl radicals to neutralize viruses, bacteria, and VOCs.

**The concern:** Ionization can produce ozone. Winix states PlasmaWave produces below 5 ppb — well under the 50 ppb CARB safety threshold.

**Our recommendation:** If you have ozone sensitivity, turn PlasmaWave off with the dedicated button. The HEPA filter alone delivers excellent particle filtration.

## Four-Stage Filtration

1. **Washable pre-filter** — Catches pet hair and large dust. Wash monthly to extend HEPA life.
2. **Washable activated carbon filter** — Absorbs odors, smoke, and VOCs. Reusable — no replacement cost.
3. **True HEPA filter** — Captures 99.99% of particles down to 0.01 microns.
4. **PlasmaWave** (optional) — Bipolar ionization for additional air treatment.

The washable carbon filter remains the 5510's biggest cost advantage. Competitors charge $15-20 every 3-6 months for carbon replacements.
