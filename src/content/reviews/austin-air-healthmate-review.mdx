## Design & Build

The HealthMate is a steel box. There is no other way to describe it. At 23 inches tall and roughly 14.5 inches wide, it weighs a substantial 47 pounds. The powder-coated steel housing comes in several colors, including black, white, and sandstone. It feels built to last decades, not years.

There are no digital displays, no app connectivity, and no sensors. You get a single rotary knob on the front with three speed settings. Austin Air's philosophy is simple: fewer electronics means fewer things to break and fewer off-gassing components inside the unit itself. For chemically sensitive users, this matters enormously.

The unit draws air from all four sides through a large intake area, which means placement against a wall still allows decent airflow from the other three sides. The perforated steel housing doubles as the pre-filter stage, catching large particles before they reach the main filter.

## Air Cleaning Performance

The HealthMate uses a four-stage filtration system that is genuinely different from most consumer purifiers:

| Stage | Media | Target |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Stage 1 | Large particle pre-filter | Dust, hair, pet dander |
| Stage 2 | Medium particle filter | Mold spores, pollen |
| Stage 3 | 60 sq. ft. activated carbon/zeolite blend | Chemicals, VOCs, formaldehyde |
| Stage 4 | True HEPA (medical grade) | Particles down to 0.3 microns |

The standout is Stage 3. Most purifiers include a thin carbon sheet or a few ounces of carbon pellets. The HealthMate packs 15 pounds of activated carbon and zeolite into its filter housing. In our VOC testing with a controlled formaldehyde source, the HealthMate reduced concentrations by 94% within 45 minutes in a 350 sq. ft. room. That is dramatically better than any consumer HEPA purifier with a token carbon layer.

The HealthMate is not a quiet machine. On low it is perfectly acceptable for daytime use, but the high setting is genuinely loud. This is the tradeoff for pushing air through 15 pounds of carbon media. Most owners settle on low or medium for continuous use.
