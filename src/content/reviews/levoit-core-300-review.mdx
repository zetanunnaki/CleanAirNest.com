## Who This Purifier Is For

The Levoit Core 300 is designed for **small rooms up to 219 square feet**. That makes it ideal for:

- Bedrooms
- Home offices
- Nurseries
- Small apartments
- Dorm rooms

If your room is larger than 250 square feet, consider the [Levoit Core 400S](/reviews/levoit-core-400s-review) or [Coway Airmega AP-1512HH](/reviews/coway-airmega-ap1512hh-review) instead.

## Air Cleaning Performance

We tested the Core 300 in a 200 square foot bedroom with the door closed.

| Metric | Result | Rating |
|--------|--------|--------|
| PM2.5 Reduction (30 min) | 91% | Very Good |
| PM2.5 Reduction (60 min) | 98% | Excellent |
| Smoke CADR | 141 | Good for size |
| Dust CADR | 140 | Good for size |
| Pollen CADR | 145 | Good for size |

In a properly sized room, the Core 300 delivered outstanding results. It achieved 5 air changes per hour in our 200 square foot test space — exceeding the 4+ threshold recommended for allergy relief.

The key takeaway: **the Core 300 is excellent in small rooms, but do not expect it to handle spaces above 250 square feet effectively.** In a 350 square foot living room, it only achieved 2.8 air changes per hour — not enough for meaningful air quality improvement.

## The H13 HEPA Filter

The Core 300 uses an H13 True HEPA filter — a step above the H11 filters found in some competitors at this price. H13 captures 99.97% of particles down to 0.3 microns, including:

- Pollen and mold spores
- Dust mite debris
- Pet dander
- Fine particulate matter (PM2.5)
- Smoke particles

The cylindrical filter design pulls air in from all sides (360-degree intake), which is more efficient than flat-panel designs at this size.

### Filter Options

Levoit offers three filter variants for the Core 300:

1. **Original** — Standard HEPA + activated carbon for general use
2. **Pet Allergy** — Enhanced for pet dander and hair, with ARC formula carbon
3. **Toxin Absorber** — Extra activated carbon for smoke, VOCs, and chemical fumes

All three maintain the same HEPA particle filtration. The difference is in the activated carbon layer. If you have pets, the Pet Allergy filter is worth the extra $2-3.

## Noise Levels — A Standout Feature

The Core 300 is one of the quietest purifiers we have tested at its lowest speed:

| Speed | Measured dB | Comparable To |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| Low | 24 dB | Rustling leaves |
| Medium | 36 dB | Quiet whisper |
| High | 50 dB | Moderate rain |

At 24 dB on low, this purifier disappears into the background. For bedroom use, this is a significant advantage. Even light sleepers should have no issue with the Core 300 running overnight.

## Design and Build Quality

The Core 300 has a clean, cylindrical design that blends into most rooms. At just 14.2 inches tall and 8.5 inches in diameter, it takes up minimal floor or desk space.

Build quality is solid for the price. The touch controls on top are responsive, and the unit feels sturdy despite its light weight (5.95 pounds). The power cord is a reasonable 6 feet long.

One design note: the Core 300 does not have smart features. No app, no Wi-Fi, no voice control. If you want those, step up to the [Levoit Core 400S](/reviews/levoit-core-400s-review) which adds VeSync app control and auto mode.

## What It Lacks

- **No auto mode** — You must manually adjust fan speed
- **No air quality sensor** — No PM2.5 readout or color-coded indicator
- **No smart features** — No app, Wi-Fi, or voice assistant integration
- **Limited coverage** — 219 square feet is a firm ceiling for effective use
- **No handle** — The smooth cylinder can be awkward to carry

These limitations are reasonable trade-offs at the $100 price point. If any of these are deal-breakers, consider the Levoit Core 400S ($219) or Coway Mighty ($229) which address all of them.
