If you suffer from seasonal allergies, pet dander, or dust mite sensitivities, a quality HEPA air purifier can dramatically reduce airborne allergens in your home. After testing over 20 purifiers in controlled conditions, these five stood out for allergy sufferers.

## What to Look For in an Allergy Air Purifier

The most important factor is **True HEPA filtration** — these filters capture 99.97% of particles down to 0.3 microns, which includes pollen, dust mite debris, mold spores, and pet dander. Avoid purifiers labeled "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like," as these use inferior filters.

You also want high CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) ratings relative to your room size, and quiet operation so the purifier can run overnight without disturbing sleep.
