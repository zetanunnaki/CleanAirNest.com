Most air purifiers are designed for a single room, but if you want to improve air quality throughout your entire home, you need a unit with serious coverage. Whole-house portable air purifiers deliver CADR ratings and coverage areas large enough to handle open-concept living spaces, multi-room apartments, or even entire floors of a house. We tested the highest-coverage purifiers available to find the five that truly deliver whole-home performance.

## What to Look For in a Whole-House Air Purifier

**Coverage area** is the defining specification. Look for purifiers rated for at least 1,000 sq. ft. — and understand that manufacturer claims assume optimal placement and open floor plans. For homes with separate rooms and hallways, you may need 20-30% more coverage than your actual square footage to account for restricted airflow.

**CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate)** tells you how quickly the purifier can clean the air. For whole-house use, you want the highest CADR possible. A CADR of 400+ cfm is the threshold for genuine whole-home effectiveness. Below that, the purifier may technically cover the square footage but take too long to cycle the air — meaning pollutants can build up faster than they are removed.

Noise is a practical concern at this scale. Whole-house purifiers push a lot of air, and the physics of moving large volumes means more fan noise. The best units manage this through aerodynamic design, oversized fans running at lower RPMs, or sound-dampening materials. Check noise levels at both maximum and medium settings, since you will likely run the unit on medium most of the time.
