**Why choose evaporative:**

## Humidifier Buying Guide: What Matters Under $100

### Tank Size and Runtime

This is the single most important practical consideration. A humidifier you must refill every few hours quickly becomes an annoyance. Here is what to expect:

| Tank Size | Approximate Runtime (Low) | Refill Frequency | Best For |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------|----------|
| 1.5 L | 20-25 hours | Daily | Bedrooms, nightstands |
| 2.0-2.5 L | 24-30 hours | Daily | Bedrooms, small living rooms |
| 4.0-6.0 L | 36-50 hours | Every 2-3 days | Living rooms, large bedrooms, offices |

For daily-use convenience, a 4L+ tank is the comfort threshold. Models with 1.5-2.5L tanks work well for overnight bedroom use but will need a morning refill.

### Ultrasonic vs. Evaporative

| Feature | Ultrasonic | Evaporative |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Noise | Near-silent (25-35 dB) | Audible fan (35-50 dB) |
| White dust | Yes, with hard tap water | No, never |
| Filters | None (no ongoing cost) | Wicking filter ($12-18/1-3 months) |
| Over-humidification risk | Yes, without a sensor | No — self-regulating |
| Warm mist option | Some models | Rare |
| Water type | Distilled recommended | Any tap water |

For silent bedroom use, ultrasonic wins. For hard-water households, evaporative eliminates the white dust issue entirely.

### Smart Features: Worth It Under $100?

At this price point, smart features are a genuine differentiator. The Levoit LV600S and Dreo 6L both include app control, humidity sensors, and auto mode — features that make daily operation genuinely hands-free. Customer reviewers who upgrade from manual to smart humidifiers consistently rank the auto humidity feature as the most valuable addition.

Without a humidity sensor, you are guessing at output levels and risking either under-humidifying (no improvement) or over-humidifying (condensation on windows, potential mold growth). A built-in sensor that maintains 40-50% humidity automatically is worth the price premium.

### Maintenance: The Non-Negotiable

Regardless of price or brand, every humidifier requires weekly cleaning. Standing water breeds bacteria and mold, which the unit then disperses into your breathing air. The EPA and multiple health organizations emphasize this point consistently.

**Weekly maintenance checklist:**
1. Empty all remaining water from the tank and base
2. Wipe the interior with white vinegar or a diluted bleach solution (1 teaspoon per gallon)
3. Rinse thoroughly with clean water
4. Allow all parts to air dry before refilling
5. For evaporative models: rinse or replace the wicking filter per the manufacturer's schedule

A well-maintained $30 humidifier will produce cleaner, healthier mist than a neglected $200 model.
