Mold spores are everywhere — outdoors they are a natural part of the ecosystem, but indoors they trigger allergies, aggravate asthma, and can cause serious health problems with prolonged exposure. An air purifier cannot fix a mold problem at its source (you still need to address moisture), but it can dramatically reduce airborne spore counts and prevent mold from spreading to new areas. We tested the top purifiers specifically for mold spore capture and neutralization.

## What to Look For in a Mold Air Purifier

**True HEPA filtration** is non-negotiable. Mold spores typically range from 1 to 30 microns in size, well within the capture range of a True HEPA filter (99.97% at 0.3 microns). However, the smaller fragments of mold — called hyphal fragments and mycotoxins — can be much smaller. For comprehensive mold protection, look for filters rated below 0.3 microns or units with additional filtration stages.

**Activated carbon** plays an important supporting role. While carbon does not capture mold spores, it adsorbs the musty, earthy volatile organic compounds (MVOCs) that mold colonies produce. These are the compounds responsible for that characteristic damp, musty smell. A purifier with a substantial carbon filter will address both the visible spores and the unpleasant odor.

Some purifiers include **UV-C light** that claims to kill captured mold spores and bacteria on the filter. While UV-C can be effective, exposure time matters — spores need prolonged UV contact for reliable inactivation. Look for units where air passes slowly through a UV chamber rather than designs where air rushes past a UV bulb at high speed.
