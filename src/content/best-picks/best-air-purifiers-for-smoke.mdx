## What to Look For in a Smoke Air Purifier

The key to smoke removal is **activated carbon filtration** — and lots of it. HEPA filters capture the visible particulate matter in smoke, but the gaseous chemicals and persistent odors pass right through. You need a thick bed of activated carbon, ideally several pounds, to adsorb the VOCs and tar compounds that create that stale smoke smell.

Avoid purifiers that rely on thin carbon mesh or carbon-coated filters. These are designed for light odors like cooking smells, not the heavy chemical load that tobacco smoke produces. Look for purifiers advertising carbon filters measured in pounds, not millimeters.

Airflow rate is also critical. Smoke disperses quickly and its ultrafine particles (many smaller than 0.1 microns) stay suspended for hours. A high CADR with strong airflow ensures smoke gets pulled into the purifier before it can settle into walls, furniture, and carpeting. Run the purifier on a higher speed setting during and immediately after smoking for best results.
