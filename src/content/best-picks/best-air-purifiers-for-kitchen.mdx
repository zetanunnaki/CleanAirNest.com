## What Makes a Kitchen Air Purifier Different

A bedroom purifier and a kitchen purifier solve fundamentally different problems. In a bedroom, you are filtering dust, allergens, and maybe some pet dander — relatively light, dry particles. In a kitchen, you are dealing with:

- **Grease aerosols** — Microscopic oil droplets generated by frying and sauteing that coat surfaces and clog standard HEPA filters faster than any other indoor pollutant
- **Cooking VOCs** — The chemical compounds responsible for lingering food smells. Garlic, fish, curry spices, and burnt oils all release VOCs that pass straight through a HEPA filter
- **Combustion byproducts** — Gas stoves produce nitrogen dioxide and carbon monoxide. Even electric stoves generate smoke particles when oil hits a hot pan
- **High-volume particle spikes** — Cooking creates sudden, dense clouds of PM2.5 that require rapid air cycling to clear before they settle on countertops, cabinets, and clothing

This means the two specs that matter most for a kitchen purifier are **activated carbon weight** (for odors and gases) and **smoke CADR** (for particle clearing speed). A purifier with a thin carbon sheet might handle a scented candle, but it will be overwhelmed by a pan of searing salmon in minutes.

## Kitchen Air Purifier Placement Tips

Where you put your purifier matters more in the kitchen than in any other room. Get it wrong and you reduce effectiveness dramatically — or worse, create a safety issue.

1. **Keep it 3-5 feet from the stove** — Close enough to intercept cooking particles before they spread through the room, but far enough to avoid grease splatters reaching the intake. Grease on a HEPA filter degrades performance and is difficult to remove.

2. **Never place it in the steam path** — Steam from boiling water can saturate the HEPA media and promote mold growth inside the filter. Position the purifier off to the side of the stove, not directly in front of or behind it where steam rises.

3. **Elevate it if possible** — Cooking smoke and heat rise. Placing the purifier on a counter or shelf (2-4 feet off the ground) puts the intake closer to where cooking pollutants concentrate, improving capture efficiency.

4. **Point the clean air outlet away from the stove** — You want the purifier pulling contaminated air in, not blowing clean air toward the cooking surface where it just picks up more pollutants and creates turbulence.

5. **Run on high before and during cooking** — Start the purifier 5-10 minutes before you begin cooking to establish airflow patterns. Switch to auto mode after you finish to let it manage the cleanup.

6. **Clean pre-filters monthly** — Kitchen grease accumulates on pre-filters 3 to 4 times faster than in bedrooms. A clogged pre-filter chokes airflow and forces the motor to work harder, increasing noise and energy consumption.
