## Why Studios Need the Right Purifier

A studio apartment is not just a small room — it is every room combined into one. This creates specific air quality challenges that matter when choosing a purifier:

**Cooking in your bedroom.** When you sear a steak or fry onions, those PM2.5 particles do not stay in the "kitchen area." In a studio, cooking smoke concentrations can reach 100-300 µg/m³ within minutes — well above the EPA's 35 µg/m³ 24-hour standard. Your purifier needs enough CADR to clear those particles before bedtime.

**Sleeping next to your purifier.** In a house, you can put a loud purifier in the living room and a quiet one in the bedroom. In a studio, there is one purifier and it needs to be quiet enough for sleep. Anything above 30 dB will disturb light sleepers. The best studio purifiers operate below 25 dB on low.

**Limited floor space.** Every square foot counts. A purifier with a large footprint or one that needs clearance on all four sides eats into your living area. Tower designs and compact cylindrical models work best in studios.

**No separate ventilation zones.** Houses have rooms with doors that contain odors and particles. Studios have one continuous air volume. Your purifier handles all pollutant sources simultaneously — pets, cooking, body odor, off-gassing furniture, and whatever comes through the windows.

**Key specs for studio apartments:**
- CADR: 150-250 (matches typical 300-500 sq. ft. studios)
- Noise on low: Under 25 dB (critical for sleep)
- Footprint: Under 15 inches in any dimension
- Air quality sensor: Essential for auto-adjusting between cooking and sleeping
- Activated carbon: Important for cooking odor absorption

## Placement Tips for Studios

Where you put your purifier matters as much as which one you buy — especially in a studio where one unit handles everything.

**Near the kitchen area, facing the living/sleeping space.** This placement catches cooking particles as they disperse toward your bed. The purifier's intake pulls contaminated air from the cooking zone, and its output pushes clean air toward where you sleep and relax.

**At least 3 feet from walls and corners.** Air purifiers need clearance for proper airflow. Pushing them into a corner reduces CADR by 20-30% according to AHAM testing guidelines.

**Off the floor if possible.** PM2.5 concentrations are often higher at breathing height (3-5 feet) than at floor level. Placing a compact purifier like the Levoit Core 300 on a side table or shelf can improve effective cleaning.

**Away from air conditioning vents.** AC airflow disrupts the purifier's intake pattern. Position your purifier at least 5 feet from supply vents.

**Do not block the output.** Make sure nothing blocks the clean air output — no curtains, blankets, or furniture within 2 feet of the exhaust.
