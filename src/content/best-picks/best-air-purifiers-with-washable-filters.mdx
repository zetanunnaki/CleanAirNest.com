Replacement filters are the hidden cost of owning an air purifier — typically $40-$80 per year, adding up to hundreds over the unit's lifetime. Air purifiers with washable or permanent filters eliminate or drastically reduce this ongoing expense. We tested the best models to find which ones deliver genuine performance without the recurring filter costs.

## What to Look For in a Washable-Filter Air Purifier

Not all washable filters are equal. The most common type is a **washable pre-filter** that captures large particles like dust, hair, and pet fur while a separate HEPA filter handles fine particulates. This extends the HEPA filter's life significantly but does not eliminate replacement costs entirely.

True permanent filters — like Honeywell's iFD technology or certain electrostatic designs — can be washed and reused indefinitely. However, they may not match True HEPA's 99.97% efficiency at 0.3 microns. For most homes, a washable pre-filter combined with a long-lasting HEPA filter is the most practical and effective approach.

Ease of maintenance is also important. A filter that requires disassembly with tools or takes days to dry is impractical for regular cleaning. The best designs pop out easily, rinse under a faucet, and dry within a few hours. Look for units with clear maintenance indicators that remind you when cleaning is due.
