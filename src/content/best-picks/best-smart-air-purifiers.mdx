Smart air purifiers let you monitor air quality, adjust settings, and automate schedules from your phone — or simply by asking Alexa or Google Assistant. But not all smart purifiers are created equal. Some have buggy apps, unreliable sensors, or limited automation options. We spent weeks testing Wi-Fi-connected purifiers to find the ones where the smart features genuinely improve the air-cleaning experience.

## What to Look For in a Smart Air Purifier

The foundation of a good smart purifier is a **reliable app with real-time air quality data**. You want to see PM2.5 readings, filter life remaining, and historical trends. The best apps also let you create schedules, set up automations (like boosting the fan when air quality drops), and receive push notifications when filters need replacing.

Voice assistant compatibility is essential for hands-free control. Most quality smart purifiers now support both Amazon Alexa and Google Assistant, with some adding Apple HomeKit or Samsung SmartThings. If you are already invested in a smart home ecosystem, check compatibility before buying.

Sensor accuracy matters as well. A smart purifier is only as good as the data driving its auto mode. Laser-based particle sensors are more accurate than infrared ones, detecting smaller particle sizes and responding faster to air quality changes. The best units combine a particle sensor with a VOC sensor for comprehensive monitoring.

## 2. Best App Experience: Winix AM90

<ProductCard productId="winix-am90" rank={2} />

The Winix AM90 delivers one of the best companion apps we have tested. The Winix Smart app provides granular control — you can set specific fan speeds for different times of day, create automation rules based on air quality readings, and view detailed air quality history in clean, easy-to-read charts.

What sets the AM90 apart is its **smart sensors**. The unit houses both a particle sensor and a gas/odor sensor, giving the auto mode a fuller picture of your indoor air quality. In testing, it responded to cooking fumes and cleaning product vapors that particle-only sensors missed entirely.

True HEPA filtration with a PlasmaWave-equipped four-stage system handles particles, odors, and VOCs. The 360 sq. ft. coverage is suitable for most bedrooms and home offices. Wi-Fi setup took under two minutes in our experience, with no connectivity drops during our month-long test period.

The Shark Air Purifier 6 covers a massive 1,200 sq. ft. while offering Clean Sense IQ — Shark's proprietary smart sensing technology that continuously monitors air quality and auto-adjusts fan speed in real time. For large homes or open-concept spaces, this is the smart purifier to choose.
