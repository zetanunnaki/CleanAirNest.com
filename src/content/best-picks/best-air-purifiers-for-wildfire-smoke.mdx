Wildfire smoke creates some of the most dangerous indoor air quality conditions homeowners face. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) from smoke can penetrate deep into your lungs, and during heavy smoke events, indoor PM2.5 levels can reach 10-20x safe limits even with windows closed.

A high-quality HEPA air purifier is your best defense. Here are the purifiers that performed best in our smoke simulation testing.

## Why Wildfire Smoke Requires Specific Purifier Features

Not all air purifiers handle smoke equally well. You need:

- **High CADR for smoke specifically** — smoke particles are the smallest (0.1-1.0 microns), so look for the Smoke CADR rating
- **Activated carbon filtration** — removes smoke odor and gaseous pollutants that HEPA filters miss
- **Fast clean rate** — during AQI spikes, you need rapid air turnover
- **Sealed system** — ensures all air passes through the filter, not around it

## Tips for Wildfire Season Preparedness

1. **Buy your purifier before fire season** — prices spike and stock runs out during active fires
2. **Stock up on replacement filters** — supply chain issues are common during prolonged smoke events
3. **Create a clean air room** — designate one room, seal it well, and run your purifier on high
4. **Monitor your air quality** — pair your purifier with an air quality monitor to know when conditions improve
