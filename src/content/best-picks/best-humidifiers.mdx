## How We Tested

We placed each humidifier in a sealed 300 sq. ft. room with starting humidity at 25%. We measured:

- **Time to reach 45% humidity** (our target)
- **Total runtime on a full tank** (lowest mist setting)
- **Noise level** at 6 feet
- **Ease of refilling and cleaning**
- **White dust production** (an issue with some ultrasonic models)

## Ultrasonic vs. Evaporative: Which Is Better?

| Feature | Ultrasonic | Evaporative |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| White dust | Yes (use distilled water) | None |
| Noise | Very quiet | Slightly louder |
| Filter cost | None | $8-15/month |
| Energy use | Very low | Low |
| Warm mist option | Some models | No |

**For most people, ultrasonic is the better choice** — quieter, no ongoing filter costs, and warm mist options available. Just use distilled or demineralized water to avoid white dust.

## Tips for Optimal Humidity

1. **Target 40-50% humidity** — Use a hygrometer or smart monitor to track levels
2. **Use distilled water in ultrasonic humidifiers** — Prevents white dust and mineral buildup
3. **Clean weekly** — Standing water breeds bacteria and mold
4. **Place on a waterproof surface** — Condensation can damage wood floors and furniture
5. **Don't over-humidify** — Above 60% humidity encourages mold growth and dust mites
