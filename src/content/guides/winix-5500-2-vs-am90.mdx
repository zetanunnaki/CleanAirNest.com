## 1. Air Cleaning Performance: A Virtual Tie

Both the 5500-2 and AM90 use the same three-stage filtration system: a washable mesh pre-filter, a True HEPA filter that captures 99.97% of particles at 0.3 microns, and Winix's PlasmaWave technology that generates hydroxyl radicals to neutralize odors and certain VOCs.

The CADR ratings tell the story — the AM90 has a marginal edge (233/246/250 vs 232/243/246 for smoke, dust, and pollen), but the difference is so small that it falls within normal testing variance. According to customer reviews, users report identical air quality results from both models. You will not notice a performance difference between these two purifiers in any real-world room.

Both cover approximately 360 sq ft with adequate air changes per hour. For a standard bedroom (150-200 sq ft), either purifier delivers outstanding performance. For a medium living room (300-360 sq ft), both maintain effective air changes without struggling.

**Bottom line:** If you are choosing between these two based on air cleaning ability, save your money. The 5500-2 cleans the air just as well.

## 2. The Smart Feature Divide

This is the only category where the AM90 meaningfully separates itself from the 5500-2 — and it is the entire reason for the $40 price premium.

### What the AM90 adds:

- **Winix Smart app** — Remote control from anywhere, real-time air quality monitoring on your phone, scheduling, and filter life tracking
- **Alexa and Google Assistant** — Voice commands for power, fan speed, and mode changes
- **Remote scheduling** — Set daily or weekly schedules through the app
- **Air quality history** — View air quality trends over time in the app

### What both purifiers share:

- **Auto mode** — Both have built-in air quality sensors that automatically adjust fan speed based on detected particle levels
- **Air quality LED indicator** — Both display a color-coded LED showing current air quality (blue for good, amber for moderate, red for poor)
- **Sleep mode** — Both dim the display and run on the lowest fan speed
- **PlasmaWave on/off toggle** — Both let you disable the ionization feature

The 5500-2 is not a "dumb" purifier. Its built-in air quality sensor and auto mode handle the most important automation task — adjusting fan speed based on actual air quality — without needing an app or Wi-Fi connection. Based on our research, the sensor in both models performs reliably, ramping up quickly when cooking fumes or dust are detected and settling back to low speed when air quality stabilizes.

The AM90's smart features are genuine conveniences, not gimmicks. Being able to check your home's air quality from your phone, turn the purifier on before arriving home, or set weekly schedules through the app adds real value. But these are convenience features, not performance features. They do not make the air any cleaner.

## 3. Filter Compatibility and Long-Term Costs

Both the 5500-2 and AM90 use the same filter type, which is a significant advantage for Winix buyers who might upgrade later or own both models. The True HEPA filter and the washable activated carbon filter are interchangeable between the two units.

The $40 purchase price gap never closes. Filter costs are identical, energy consumption is nearly identical, and the washable pre-filter and carbon filter in both models reduce ongoing expenses compared to purifiers that require disposable carbon replacements.

Over three years, the 5500-2 saves you roughly $40 compared to the AM90. That $40 bought you Wi-Fi connectivity and app control — a reasonable price for those features if you will actually use them.

**Important note on replacement filters:** Both models use widely available HEPA replacement filters that can be purchased on Amazon and other retailers. Winix also sells filter bundles that bring the per-filter cost down. The washable carbon filter is a standout feature on both — most competing purifiers in this price range require $15-20 disposable carbon filter replacements every 3-6 months.

## 4. Noise Levels: Essentially Identical

Based on published specifications and customer feedback, both purifiers produce similar noise levels across their fan speed range. On the lowest setting, both operate at approximately 28 dB — quiet enough for bedroom use, though not as whisper-silent as some competitors like the Coway AP-1512HH at 24 dB.

On maximum speed, both reach approximately 54-55 dB, which is comparable to a normal conversation. This is typical for mid-range purifiers with CADR ratings in the 230-250 range.

Sleep mode on both models dims the LED display and locks the fan at the lowest speed, making either purifier suitable for overnight bedroom use. According to customer reviews, the vast majority of users find both models quiet enough for sleeping, though a few light sleepers note a slight hum on low.

## 5. Design and Build Quality

The 5500-2 and AM90 share a similar design language — both are rectangular tower-style purifiers with top-mounted controls, front-facing air quality indicators, and a dark exterior. The AM90 is slightly more refined in its design details, with a marginally slimmer profile and updated control panel, but the differences are subtle.

Both purifiers are well-built based on customer feedback, with solid construction that holds up over years of daily use. Neither feels premium, but both feel reliable — exactly what you want in a mid-range appliance.

The 5500-2 weighs 15.4 lbs and the AM90 weighs 15 lbs. Both are easy to move between rooms with one hand.

## 6. PlasmaWave: What You Should Know

Both models include Winix's PlasmaWave bipolar ionization technology, which generates hydroxyl radicals to help break down odors, allergens, and some chemical vapors on a molecular level. Winix states that PlasmaWave produces no harmful ozone, and independent testing has generally confirmed that ozone output falls well below the California CARB safety threshold.

That said, if anyone in your household has severe asthma, respiratory sensitivities, or if you keep pet birds, it is worth disabling PlasmaWave on either model. Both purifiers work perfectly well as pure mechanical HEPA + carbon filters with PlasmaWave turned off. The dedicated button on both models makes this easy — press once to disable, press again to enable.

## Who Should Buy Which

### Buy the Winix AM90 ($200) if:

- You want to control your purifier from your phone when away from home
- You use Alexa or Google Assistant and want voice control integration
- You value app-based scheduling and air quality history tracking
- You are building a connected smart home ecosystem
- The $40 premium is not a concern for the added convenience

### Buy the Winix 5500-2 ($160) if:

- You do not care about app control or voice assistants
- You want the lowest-cost Winix with full auto mode functionality
- Budget is your primary concern
- You prefer a simpler setup with no Wi-Fi configuration required
- You want the same air cleaning performance and plan to use auto mode on the unit itself

## The Bottom Line

The Winix 5500-2 and AM90 deliver the same clean air. Period. The PlasmaWave technology, HEPA filtration, CADR ratings, room coverage, noise levels, and filter costs are virtually identical. The $40 question comes down to one thing: do you want to control your purifier from your phone?

If the answer is yes — if you want remote access, app scheduling, air quality history, and voice assistant integration — the AM90 is worth $40 more. These are genuinely useful smart features, and $40 is a reasonable price for them.

If the answer is no — if auto mode on the unit itself is all the automation you need — save your $40 and buy the 5500-2. You get the exact same air cleaning performance without the connectivity you will never use.

For most buyers, the 5500-2 at $160 is the better value. The built-in auto mode handles the most important job (adjusting fan speed to actual air quality) without an app. The AM90 is the smarter splurge for smart home enthusiasts who want everything connected.
