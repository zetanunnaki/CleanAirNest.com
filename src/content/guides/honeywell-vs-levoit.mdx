## CADR and Performance: Honeywell's Raw Power Advantage

The Honeywell HPA300 boasts a smoke CADR of 300 — among the highest you will find in any air purifier under $250. This translates to genuinely faster air cleaning in larger rooms. According to customer reviews, the HPA300 can noticeably reduce cooking odors and smoke within minutes when running on its highest setting.

The Levoit Core 400S delivers a smoke CADR of 260 — roughly 15% lower. In rooms under 350 sq. ft., this difference is marginal in real-world use. Both purifiers achieve clean air quickly. The gap becomes more meaningful in larger spaces (400+ sq. ft.), where the Honeywell's higher airflow provides a noticeable edge.

| Performance Metric | Honeywell HPA300 | Levoit Core 400S |
|--------------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Smoke CADR** | 300 | 260 |
| **Estimated ACH (300 sq. ft.)** | 6.4 | 5.5 |
| **Estimated ACH (400 sq. ft.)** | 4.8 | 4.2 |
| **Effective max room size (4+ ACH)** | ~465 sq. ft. | ~403 sq. ft. |

**Key takeaway:** If you have a large open room (400+ sq. ft.) and want the fastest possible air cleaning, the Honeywell HPA300 has the edge. For rooms under 350 sq. ft., both perform more than adequately.

The Levoit Core 400S does have one performance advantage: its laser PM2.5 sensor enables true auto mode, which adjusts fan speed based on measured air quality. The Honeywell has no air quality sensing at all — you manually select a fan speed and it runs at that speed regardless of conditions.

## Noise: Levoit Wins by a Wide Margin

This is the single largest practical difference between these two purifiers, and it is not close.

| Setting | Honeywell HPA300 | Levoit Core 400S |
|---------|------------------|------------------|
| **Lowest** | ~42 dB | 24 dB |
| **Medium** | ~52 dB | ~40 dB |
| **Highest** | ~64 dB | 52 dB |

The Honeywell HPA300 is notoriously loud. At 42 dB on its lowest setting, it is louder than many purifiers on their medium settings. On its highest "Turbo" speed, 64 dB is comparable to a normal conversation — you will hear it across the room and it will compete with your television.

The Levoit Core 400S at 24 dB on its sleep setting is essentially silent. Most people cannot distinguish it from background room noise. Even on high (52 dB), it is quieter than the Honeywell on medium.

**For bedroom use, the Levoit is the only realistic option.** Customer reviews for the Honeywell HPA300 consistently mention noise as the primary complaint, with many users noting they cannot use it on anything above the lowest speed while sleeping. The Levoit receives widespread praise for quiet operation, with reviewers frequently calling it "whisper quiet" or "inaudible" on low.

If you plan to use a purifier in a living room while watching television, the Honeywell's noise may be manageable on low. In a bedroom, nursery, or home office where quiet matters, the Levoit Core 400S is dramatically better.

## Smart Features: Levoit in a Different League

The Levoit Core 400S includes a full suite of modern smart home features:

- **VeSync app** — Real-time PM2.5 readings, air quality history, filter life tracking, and remote control
- **Laser PM2.5 sensor** — Accurate particle detection that drives true auto mode
- **Auto mode** — Adjusts fan speed based on measured air quality in real time
- **Scheduling** — Program on/off times and fan speeds by day
- **Alexa and Google Assistant** — Voice commands for hands-free control
- **Sleep mode** — Display off, minimum fan speed, auto mode disabled

The Honeywell HPA300 has none of this. No app. No Wi-Fi. No voice control. No air quality sensor. No auto mode. It has five manually selected fan speeds and a timer. That is the extent of its "features."

This is a generational gap. The Honeywell HPA300 was designed in an era before smart home integration became standard, and Honeywell has not updated the model. For users who want to monitor their air quality, adjust settings remotely, or integrate their purifier into a smart home ecosystem, the Honeywell simply cannot compete.

**If you do not care about smart features** — if you just want to set a fan speed and leave it — the Honeywell's simplicity is not a downside. Some users prefer a straightforward appliance without apps. But most buyers in 2026 expect at least basic app control, and the Levoit delivers it.

## Filter Costs: The Long Game Favors Levoit Significantly

This is where the Honeywell's hidden expense becomes apparent.

| Cost Item | Honeywell HPA300 | Levoit Core 400S |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| **Replacement HEPA filter** | ~$55-60 (pack) | ~$25-30 |
| **Replacement pre-filter** | ~$30-40 (pack of 4) | Included with HEPA |
| **Filter replacement frequency** | Every 12 months | Every 6-8 months |
| **Annual filter cost** | ~$90-100 | ~$35 |

The Honeywell HPA300 uses a separate HEPA filter and carbon pre-filter, both of which need regular replacement. The HEPA filter runs approximately $55-60, and the pre-filter pack costs $30-40. Together, annual filter costs approach $90-100 depending on usage.

The Levoit Core 400S uses a single integrated HEPA H13 filter with an activated carbon layer, priced at approximately $25-30. It needs replacement roughly every 6-8 months, which translates to approximately $35 per year.

Over three years, this adds up to a substantial difference.

According to customer reviews, several Honeywell HPA300 owners express frustration about the ongoing filter costs after learning about the expense post-purchase.

## Brand Trust: Honeywell's One Remaining Edge

Honeywell is a household name with decades of appliance manufacturing behind it. The HPA300 carries a 5-year limited warranty — significantly longer than Levoit's 2-year coverage. For buyers who value brand heritage and the assurance of a long warranty, this matters.

Levoit is a newer brand that built its reputation primarily through Amazon. While customer reviews are overwhelmingly positive and the brand has earned trust through consistent product quality, it does not carry the same institutional weight as Honeywell. That said, Levoit has established itself as one of the most popular air purifier brands in the market, with millions of units sold and a responsive customer service team.

Based on customer reviews, both brands receive comparable satisfaction ratings. The Honeywell HPA300 is praised for durability and filtration power, while common complaints focus on noise and filter costs. The Levoit Core 400S is praised for quiet operation, smart features, and value, while occasional complaints mention the VeSync app requiring updates or the sensor needing periodic calibration.

## The Verdict

**For most buyers, the Levoit Core 400S is the better purchase.** It delivers smart features the Honeywell cannot match, operates at noise levels the Honeywell cannot approach, and costs roughly $233 less over three years. The CADR difference (260 vs 300) is only meaningful in rooms above 400 sq. ft. — and in those spaces, you should probably consider a larger purifier anyway.

**Buy the Honeywell HPA300 if:** You need the highest possible CADR under $250 for a large room (400-465 sq. ft.), you do not care about noise or smart features, and you value the 5-year warranty and Honeywell's brand heritage. It remains a capable air purifier with genuine cleaning power — its limitations are noise, operating costs, and a lack of modern features.

**Buy the Levoit Core 400S if:** You want a quiet, smart, efficient purifier for rooms up to 400 sq. ft. that costs less to operate over time. It is the better all-around value for bedrooms, apartments, and medium-sized living spaces. The app control, laser PM2.5 sensor, and auto mode are features you will genuinely use and appreciate.

**Buy the Levoit Core 300 if:** Your room is under 200 sq. ft. and you want the most affordable path to clean air. At $99, it delivers excellent filtration in small spaces and is quieter and cheaper to run than the Honeywell.
