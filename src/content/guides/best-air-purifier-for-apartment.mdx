## Why Apartments Need Air Purifiers

Apartments face unique air quality challenges:

- **Shared HVAC systems** circulate pollutants from other units
- **Hallway air** seeps under doors — cooking odors, cleaning chemicals, cigarette smoke
- **Poor ventilation** — many apartments lack adequate fresh air intake
- **Street-level exposure** — ground-floor apartments get more vehicle exhaust and pollen
- **Small volumes** — a 500 sq. ft. apartment concentrates particles faster than a 2,000 sq. ft. house
- **No control over neighbors** — their smoking, cooking, and cleaning affects your air

The good news: small spaces are easier to purify. A single well-chosen purifier can clean an entire studio or 1-bedroom apartment effectively.

## Apartment Air Quality Tips

### Seal Door Gaps
Cigarette smoke and cooking odors from neighbors enter primarily under your front door. A $10 door sweep or draft stopper blocks most of this infiltration and immediately improves the effectiveness of your air purifier.

### Close Windows Strategically
In ground-floor apartments near busy streets, keeping windows closed during rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM) prevents vehicle exhaust from entering. Open windows during low-traffic periods for ventilation, then close and run the purifier.

### Use Exhaust Fans
Run your bathroom exhaust fan during and after showers, and your range hood during cooking. This removes moisture and cooking particles at the source before they spread through the apartment.

### Monitor Your Air
An [air quality monitor](/best-picks/best-air-quality-monitors) helps identify when your apartment air is worst — and what causes it. Many apartment dwellers are surprised to learn that their worst air quality comes from a specific neighbor's cooking or smoking schedule.

### Talk to Your Landlord
If your apartment has persistent air quality issues from shared HVAC or building ventilation, request MERV 13 filters in the HVAC system. This is a reasonable maintenance request that benefits all tenants.
