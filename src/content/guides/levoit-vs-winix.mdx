## Brand Overview

### Levoit

Levoit, owned by Vesync, became the best-selling air purifier brand on Amazon by offering strong specs at aggressive price points. Their lineup ranges from the $100 Core 300 to the $220 Core 400S, with the Vital 200S sitting at a remarkable $90 for 380 sq. ft. of coverage. Levoit purifiers use H13 HEPA filtration (a higher-grade classification than standard True HEPA) and increasingly integrate smart features through the VeSync app. Notably, Levoit does not use any ionization technology — every model is a pure mechanical filter, which means zero ozone production.

### Winix

Winix is a South Korean manufacturer with over 40 years of air treatment experience. Their purifiers combine True HEPA filtration with the proprietary PlasmaWave technology, which generates hydroxyl radicals to neutralize odors and some airborne chemicals. The 5500-2 has been a perennial bestseller at $160, and the AM90 adds Wi-Fi connectivity for $200. Winix purifiers include washable pre-filters and washable carbon filters on some models, which reduces long-term consumable costs.

**Key takeaway:** The Levoit Core 400S leads on raw CADR, but the Winix 5500-2 offers the best balance of performance and price for mid-size rooms. The Levoit Vital 200S delivers surprisingly strong value — 380 sq. ft. of coverage for just $90 — though its CADR suggests it works best in rooms under 300 sq. ft. for optimal air changes.

The top three models (Core 400S, AM90, 5500-2) are all close enough in CADR that real-world performance differences are minimal. Customer reviews consistently confirm that all three clear a 300 sq. ft. room effectively.

## Smart Features: Levoit Takes the Lead

Smart connectivity is where Levoit has a clear advantage — particularly with the Core 400S.

### Levoit Core 400S (Best in Class)
- **VeSync app** with real-time PM2.5 readings, air quality history, and scheduling
- **Laser PM2.5 sensor** — precise particle detection, not just a basic dust sensor
- **True auto mode** that adjusts fan speed based on measured PM2.5
- **Alexa and Google Assistant** voice control
- **Filter life tracking** with remaining percentage in the app

### Levoit Vital 200S (Basic Smart)
- **VeSync app** with basic controls and scheduling
- No PM2.5 sensor — auto mode uses a simpler light-based sensor
- Alexa and Google Assistant compatible
- Solid smart functionality for its $90 price point

### Winix AM90 (Wi-Fi Enabled)
- **Winix Smart app** with remote control and scheduling

Neither offers app connectivity. The 5500-2 has a built-in air quality sensor with auto mode (adjusts fan speed automatically), which partially compensates. The Core 300 is manual-only.

**If smart features are a priority, the Levoit Core 400S is the clear winner.** Its laser PM2.5 sensor and VeSync app are meaningfully better than anything Winix offers. If you want basic smart functionality on a budget, the Vital 200S at $90 is hard to beat.

## PlasmaWave: The Winix Ionization Question

Every Winix purifier includes PlasmaWave technology — a bipolar ionization system that generates hydroxyl radicals to break down odors, allergens, and some volatile organic compounds on a molecular level. Winix markets this as a safe, ozone-free technology, and independent testing generally confirms that ozone production stays below California's CARB standard of 0.050 ppm.

**However, there are legitimate reasons for caution:**

- Any ionization process can produce trace amounts of ozone, even at levels below regulatory thresholds
- People with asthma, respiratory sensitivities, or birds in the household should avoid even trace ozone exposure
- The long-term effects of hydroxyl radical exposure in indoor environments are not fully studied

**The good news:** PlasmaWave can be turned off with a button press on both the 5500-2 and AM90. With PlasmaWave disabled, Winix purifiers function as standard HEPA + carbon mechanical filters — no ionization, no ozone, no controversy.

**No Levoit purifier in this comparison uses any form of ionization.** If you want zero ozone concerns without having to remember to disable a feature, Levoit is the safer default choice.

## The Verdict

After comparing all five models across every factor that matters, here are our specific recommendations:

**Best overall value: [Winix 5500-2](/reviews/winix-5500-2-review) ($160).** It delivers 232 CADR, covers 360 sq. ft., includes auto mode, and costs $322 over 3 years. For the majority of buyers who want a reliable, set-it-and-forget-it purifier for a living room or bedroom, this is the one to get. Just turn off PlasmaWave if you have respiratory sensitivities.

**Best smart purifier: [Levoit Core 400S](/reviews/levoit-core-400s-review) ($220).** If you want real-time PM2.5 monitoring, app control, and the highest CADR in this comparison (256), the Core 400S justifies its premium. The laser PM2.5 sensor and VeSync app are significantly better than anything Winix offers. Worth it for tech-forward buyers and those who want data on their air quality.

**Best budget pick: [Levoit Vital 200S](/reviews/levoit-vital-200s-review) ($90).** The most underrated purifier in this comparison. At $90, it covers 380 sq. ft. with basic smart app support and the lowest 3-year ownership cost ($210). If you do not need a PM2.5 sensor or the highest CADR, this is an exceptional deal.

**Best for small bedrooms: [Levoit Core 300](/reviews/levoit-core-300-review) ($100).** For rooms under 200 sq. ft., spending more is unnecessary. The Core 300 is whisper-quiet at 24 dB, delivers 5+ ACH in small rooms, and costs just $235 over 3 years. It is the best-selling air purifier on Amazon for a reason.

**Best Winix with Wi-Fi: [Winix AM90](/reviews/winix-am90-review) ($200).** If you want Winix's build quality and PlasmaWave technology with app control, the AM90 is the only Winix option here with Wi-Fi. At $200 it undercuts the Core 400S by $20, though its smart features are less refined.
