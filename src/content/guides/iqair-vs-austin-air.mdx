## 1. Filtration Technology

**Winner: IQAir for particles / Austin Air for gases**

This is the defining difference between these two purifiers, and understanding it is critical to making the right purchase.

### IQAir HyperHEPA: The Particle Specialist

The IQAir HealthPro Plus uses a three-stage filtration system engineered in Switzerland:

1. **PreMax Pre-Filter** — Captures large particles (pollen, pet dander, dust) and extends the life of the main filter.
2. **V5-Cell Gas & Odor Filter** — Contains activated carbon and alumina pellets for gas-phase filtration. At approximately 2.5 lbs of media, it handles moderate VOCs and odors but is not the V5-Cell's primary purpose.
3. **HyperHEPA Filter** — The centerpiece. This filter is certified to capture particles down to 0.003 microns at greater than 99.5% efficiency. Standard HEPA filters are rated to 0.3 microns. IQAir's HyperHEPA goes 100 times smaller.

Why does 0.003 microns matter? At that scale, you are capturing ultrafine particles — combustion byproducts from traffic and cooking, certain virus particles, nanoparticles from printers and electronics, and the smallest components of wildfire smoke. Standard HEPA filters are excellent at 0.3 microns and above, but their efficiency drops for particles in the 0.01-0.1 micron range. IQAir's HyperHEPA was designed specifically to address this gap.

The HealthPro Plus also uses a sealed system with individual filter testing — every unit ships with a hand-signed test certificate showing actual measured performance.

### Austin Air HealthMate: The Gas and Chemical Specialist

The Austin Air HealthMate HM400 uses a four-stage filtration system manufactured in Buffalo, New York:

1. **Large Particle Pre-Filter** — Removes dust and large allergens.
2. **Medium Particle Pre-Filter** — Captures smaller particulates before the main filters.
3. **15 lbs Activated Carbon + Zeolite Blend** — This is Austin Air's standout feature. Fifteen pounds of activated carbon mixed with zeolite creates an extraordinarily deep bed for gas-phase adsorption. Most consumer purifiers include a few ounces of carbon in a thin pad. Austin Air uses orders of magnitude more. This bed adsorbs VOCs, formaldehyde, benzene, toluene, ammonia, and hundreds of other gaseous pollutants.
4. **True HEPA Filter** — Certified 99.97% efficiency at 0.3 microns. This is standard (not enhanced) HEPA, but it is genuine medical-grade filtration that meets the same standard used in hospital rooms.

The zeolite component is worth noting — it is particularly effective at adsorbing formaldehyde and ammonia, which standard activated carbon handles less efficiently.

**The bottom line:** If your air quality problem is particulate (allergens, dust, smoke particles, viruses, ultrafine pollution), the IQAir's HyperHEPA is in a class by itself. If your problem is gaseous (new home off-gassing, chemical sensitivities, VOCs from paint or furniture, cooking odors, tobacco or wildfire smoke odor), Austin Air's 15-lb carbon bed is unmatched.

## 2. CADR and Airflow

**Winner: Austin Air HealthMate**

| Metric | IQAir HealthPro Plus | Austin Air HealthMate HM400 |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **CADR** | 300+ CFM (Swiss-tested) | ~400 CFM (estimated) |
| **Room Coverage** | 1,125 sq ft | 1,500 sq ft |
| **Recommended for 4+ ACH** | ~700 sq ft | ~900 sq ft |

The Austin Air HealthMate moves more air. Its estimated CADR of approximately 400 CFM gives it roughly 30% more airflow than the IQAir's 300+ CFM rating. For large rooms and open floor plans, this translates to faster air turnover and larger effective coverage.

A note on CADR ratings: Neither IQAir nor Austin Air participates in the AHAM Verifide CADR certification program. IQAir publishes its own Swiss-tested airflow numbers. Austin Air's CADR is estimated based on airflow specifications and independent assessments. Neither brand submits to the standardized third-party testing that brands like Coway, Blueair, and Levoit use. This makes direct CADR comparison less precise than with AHAM-certified models.

For practical purposes at the recommended 4+ air changes per hour, the IQAir is well-suited for large bedrooms and living rooms up to about 700 sq ft. The Austin Air can handle larger spaces approaching 900 sq ft.

## 3. Carbon Capacity and Gas-Phase Filtration

**Winner: Austin Air HealthMate (by a wide margin)**

This is not close. The Austin Air HealthMate's 15 lbs of activated carbon and zeolite is the largest gas-phase filter bed available in any residential air purifier. For context:

| Purifier | Carbon/Gas-Phase Media |
|----------|----------------------|
| Austin Air HealthMate HM400 | **15 lbs** |
| IQAir HealthPro Plus (V5-Cell) | ~2.5 lbs |
| Typical consumer purifier | 0.2-0.5 lbs |

Austin Air delivers roughly 6x the carbon media of the IQAir and 30-75x what most consumer purifiers offer. This matters because activated carbon adsorption is a capacity-based process — the carbon gets saturated over time, and more carbon means more capacity and longer effective life. Austin Air's massive carbon bed is why the company can rate its filters for 5 years of continuous use.

The IQAir V5-Cell is not a bad gas filter. It contains a meaningful amount of activated carbon and alumina pellets, and it will handle moderate odors and light VOC levels. But it was designed as a complementary stage in a particle-focused system, not as a primary chemical remediation tool.

**If you are dealing with any of the following, Austin Air's carbon capacity is the decisive advantage:**
- New construction or renovation off-gassing
- Multiple chemical sensitivity (MCS)
- Proximity to industrial or agricultural operations
- Heavy cooking odors
- Tobacco or cannabis smoke
- Wildfire smoke (odor component, not just particles)
- Formaldehyde from pressed-wood furniture or flooring

## 4. Noise Levels

**Winner: IQAir HealthPro Plus**

| Setting | IQAir HealthPro Plus | Austin Air HealthMate HM400 |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Low** | 22 dB | 35 dB |
| **High** | 59 dB | 65 dB |

The IQAir is significantly quieter at every speed setting. At 22 dB on low, it is barely audible — close to the threshold of human hearing in a quiet room. This makes the HealthPro Plus an excellent bedroom purifier that can run overnight without disrupting sleep.

The Austin Air HealthMate starts at 35 dB on its lowest setting. That is a noticeable hum — not loud, but clearly present. Most users describe it as gentle white noise. On its highest setting, the Austin Air reaches 65 dB, which is roughly the volume of a normal conversation and too loud for comfortable sleeping.

The noise difference comes down to engineering priorities. The IQAir uses a precision fan motor with vibration dampening and aerodynamic airflow channels designed in Switzerland to minimize turbulence noise. The Austin Air prioritizes raw airflow volume and carbon capacity over acoustic refinement.

**For bedroom use:** The IQAir is the clear choice. Its 22 dB low setting is essentially silent.

**For daytime use in common areas:** Both are acceptable on low and medium settings. The Austin Air's white noise may even be desirable for some users.

## 5. Build Quality and Construction

**Winner: Tie — different philosophies, both excellent**

### IQAir HealthPro Plus
Swiss-engineered and manufactured with the precision you would expect from the country that makes surgical instruments and fine watches. The housing is a durable ABS polymer with a clean, modern tower design. Internal components are machined to tight tolerances. The German EBM-Papst fan motor comes with a 10-year warranty — a statement of confidence in the engineering.

The HealthPro Plus includes caster wheels for mobility, which is helpful given its 35 lb weight. Controls are on the top panel with an intuitive interface. There is a filter life monitor that tracks actual usage hours.

At 35 lbs, it is heavy but manageable. The tower form factor is relatively compact for its performance class.

### Austin Air HealthMate HM400
Made in Buffalo, New York from 22-gauge steel — the same grade used in commercial HVAC equipment. This is an industrial-strength housing that will last decades. Where most purifiers use lightweight plastics, Austin Air builds its units like commercial equipment.

The perforated steel intake on all sides allows 360-degree air intake. The baked powder-coat finish is available in multiple colors and resists scratches and scuffs. Austin Air units from the 1990s are still running with their original housings.

At 47 lbs, the HealthMate is genuinely heavy. It does not have wheels (though aftermarket caster platforms are available). Moving it between rooms requires effort. Once you place it, expect it to stay there.

**The IQAir feels like a precision instrument. The Austin Air feels like a piece of industrial equipment. Both are built to last 10+ years.**

## 8. Who Should Buy Which

### Buy the IQAir HealthPro Plus if:
- You have severe allergies, asthma, or respiratory conditions triggered by particulates
- You need the highest possible particle filtration (0.003 microns)
- Virus and ultrafine particle removal is a priority
- You live near highways or in areas with combustion-related pollution
- Quiet bedroom operation is important (22 dB on low)
- You want Swiss precision engineering with a 10-year motor warranty
- An immunocompromised family member needs the cleanest possible air

### Buy the Austin Air HealthMate HM400 if:
- You have chemical sensitivities (MCS) or react to VOCs
- Your home has new construction, renovations, or new furniture off-gassing formaldehyde
- You live near agricultural or industrial sources of chemical pollution
- You want the strongest smoke odor removal available
- Lower long-term costs and 5-year filter life appeal to you
- You want a tank-like steel build that will last decades
- You need to cover a larger room (up to 1,500 sq ft rated)
- Budget is a consideration — lower purchase price and lower filter costs

### Consider both if:
- You have both severe particle sensitivities and chemical sensitivities. Some users with serious health conditions run one IQAir and one Austin Air in different rooms — the IQAir in the bedroom for overnight particle removal, and the Austin Air in the main living area for daytime chemical filtration. This is a significant investment ($1,600+) but provides comprehensive coverage that no single purifier can match.

## The Bottom Line

**IQAir wins on particle filtration.** The HyperHEPA technology is in a class by itself — no other residential purifier captures particles down to 0.003 microns. For allergies, asthma, virus concerns, and ultrafine pollution, the HealthPro Plus is the best purifier you can buy. It is also significantly quieter, making it the better bedroom purifier.

**Austin Air wins on gas and chemical removal.** Fifteen pounds of activated carbon is not a marketing number — it is a genuine engineering commitment to gas-phase filtration that no competitor matches. For VOCs, formaldehyde, chemical sensitivities, and persistent odors, the HealthMate HM400 is the gold standard. Its 5-year filter life and lower total cost of ownership make it the more economical long-term investment.

**Neither purifier has smart features** — no apps, no Wi-Fi, no auto mode. Both are manual, no-frills machines designed to do one thing exceptionally well. If you want app control and air quality sensors, these are not the purifiers for you. If you want the best possible filtration performance regardless of bells and whistles, you are looking at the right two machines.
