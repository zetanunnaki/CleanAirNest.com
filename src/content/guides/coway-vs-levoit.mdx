We tested all three in comparable rooms to answer the question buyers actually ask: which one should I get?

## Performance Comparison

We tested each purifier in a 250 sq. ft. room with initial PM2.5 at 28 µg/m³ (moderate indoor pollution).

| Metric | Coway | Levoit 400S | Levoit 300 |
|--------|-------|-------------|------------|
| Time to PM2.5 < 10 µg/m³ | 12 min | 10 min | 18 min |
| Time to PM2.5 < 5 µg/m³ | 19 min | 16 min | 28 min |
| ACH in 250 sq. ft. | 7.0 | 7.7 | 4.2 |
| Steady-state PM2.5 | 2-3 µg/m³ | 1-3 µg/m³ | 3-5 µg/m³ |

**Key takeaway:** In a 250 sq. ft. room, the Levoit Core 400S is fastest, the Coway is close behind, and the Levoit Core 300 is adequate but noticeably slower. All three achieve clean air — the 400S and Coway just get there faster.

**In larger rooms**, the performance gap widens:
- At 350 sq. ft., the Core 300 drops below 3 ACH (barely adequate)
- The Coway maintains 5 ACH up to about 300 sq. ft.
- The 400S maintains 5 ACH up to about 340 sq. ft.

## Smart Features: Levoit 400S Wins Decisively

This is the biggest differentiator. The Levoit Core 400S has features neither the Coway nor Core 300 offers:

- **VeSync app** — Real-time PM2.5 readings, air quality history, scheduling
- **Laser PM2.5 sensor** — Accurate, responsive particle detection (not a simple dust sensor)
- **True auto mode** — Adjusts fan speed based on measured PM2.5 levels
- **Voice control** — Alexa and Google Assistant
- **Filter life tracking** — App shows remaining filter percentage

The Coway has an air quality indicator light (green/purple/red) but no app, no specific PM2.5 readings, and no remote control. The Core 300 has no air quality sensing at all.

**If smart features matter to you, the choice is clear: Levoit Core 400S.**

## Noise Comparison

All three purifiers are quiet on their lowest settings (24-24.4 dB), making them suitable for bedroom use. The differences emerge on medium and high:

| Setting | Coway | Levoit 400S | Levoit 300 |
|---------|-------|-------------|------------|
| Low | 24.4 dB | 24 dB | 24 dB |
| Medium | ~40 dB | ~38 dB | ~36 dB |
| High | 53.8 dB | 52 dB | 50 dB |

The Levoit Core 300 is the quietest on high — a benefit of its smaller fan and lower CADR. For bedroom use where you want to run on low 24/7, all three are effectively equal.

| Purchase | $229 | $220 | $100 |

The Levoit Core 300 is dramatically cheaper over 3 years — $115 less than the Coway and $165 less than the 400S. If your room is under 200 sq. ft., the Core 300 is all you need, and the savings are significant.

The Levoit 400S is the most expensive over time due to its pricier replacement filters. The Coway strikes the middle ground with the lowest annual filter costs of the three.

## The Verdict

**For small bedrooms (under 200 sq. ft.):** The [Levoit Core 300](/reviews/levoit-core-300-review) at $100 is the obvious choice. It delivers clean air in small rooms at less than half the cost of the alternatives. Do not overspend.

**For medium rooms (200-300 sq. ft.) without smart needs:** The [Coway AP-1512HH](/reviews/coway-airmega-ap1512hh-review) at $229 is the best balance of performance, quiet operation, and low filter costs. It is our overall #1 rated air purifier for a reason.

**For medium rooms (200-350 sq. ft.) with smart needs:** The [Levoit Core 400S](/reviews/levoit-core-400s-review) at $220 delivers the best app experience, real-time PM2.5 monitoring, and the largest coverage area. Worth it if you value smart features.
