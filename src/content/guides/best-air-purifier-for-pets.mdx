## What Pet Owners Need in an Air Purifier

Not every air purifier is equally suited for pet homes. Here is what matters most:

1. **True HEPA filtration** — Captures 99.97% of pet dander particles at 0.3 microns
2. **Activated carbon filter** — Absorbs pet odors that HEPA cannot catch
3. **Strong CADR** — Pets generate particles continuously; you need high airflow
4. **Washable pre-filter** — Pet hair clogs filters fast; a washable pre-filter saves money
5. **Quiet operation** — Loud purifiers can stress noise-sensitive pets

## Pet-Specific Tips for Air Purifier Use

### Placement
- Place the purifier in the room where your pet spends the most time
- Keep the intake vent at floor level if possible — dander settles downward
- Do not block the intake with pet beds or furniture

### Maintenance
- **Clean the pre-filter weekly** — pet hair accumulates fast
- **Vacuum around the purifier** — hair collects near the intake
- **Replace HEPA filters on schedule** — pet homes may need earlier replacement

### Multi-Pet Homes
If you have multiple pets, consider running purifiers in multiple rooms rather than one large unit. Two Levoit Core 300 units ($200 total) can cover more area than one large purifier while providing better localized cleaning near where each pet rests.

### Bird Owners: Important Safety Note
If you have birds, **avoid any purifier with ionization or ozone-producing features**. Birds have extremely sensitive respiratory systems. Stick to pure mechanical HEPA filtration only:
- **Safe:** Coway AP-1512HH (turn off ionizer), Levoit Core 300, Levoit Core 400S
- **Use with caution:** Winix 5500-2 (disable PlasmaWave), Blueair 211+ (uses ionization)
- **Avoid:** Any ozone generator or UV-C purifier
