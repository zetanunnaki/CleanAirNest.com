Both use H13 HEPA filters. Both target large rooms. But they get there very differently.

## 1. CADR and Room Coverage

**Winner: Coway Airmega 400S**

The CADR gap between these two purifiers is meaningful. The Coway Airmega 400S delivers a CADR of 400+ CFM, while the Medify MA-40 comes in at 330 CFM. That is roughly 20% more air-cleaning capacity from the Coway, and in practice, the difference is noticeable in larger spaces.

The room coverage ratings tell an even bigger story. The Coway is rated for up to 1,560 sq ft — making it one of the largest-coverage purifiers available without stepping into commercial equipment territory. The Medify MA-40 is rated for 840 sq ft, which is still generous for its price but less than half the Coway's reach.

For context, at 4 Air Changes Per Hour (the threshold recommended for allergy relief):
- The Coway Airmega 400S handles rooms up to approximately 750-800 sq ft effectively
- The Medify MA-40 handles rooms up to approximately 500-550 sq ft effectively

Both purifiers are rated at higher coverage numbers because manufacturers often use 2 ACH for their ratings. But for meaningful allergy and asthma relief, 4+ ACH is the standard our editorial research recommends.

**Bottom line:** If your space exceeds 550 sq ft, the Coway Airmega 400S is the substantially stronger choice. For rooms under 500 sq ft, both purifiers deliver sufficient air changes per hour for effective purification.

## 2. Filtration Technology

**Winner: Tie — both use H13 HEPA**

Both the Medify MA-40 and the Coway Airmega 400S use H13 True HEPA filtration, which captures 99.97% of particles at 0.3 microns. This includes pollen, dust mites, pet dander, mold spores, and most bacteria. Neither has a meaningful filtration advantage over the other at the particle level.

### Medify MA-40 Filtration

The Medify uses a 3-stage filtration system: a pre-filter for large particles, an activated carbon layer for odors and VOCs, and the H13 HEPA filter. Its dual-intake design pulls air from both sides of the unit simultaneously, which creates a wider intake zone and helps clean air more uniformly in the immediate area around the purifier. According to customer reviews, this dual-sided approach is effective at reducing the perception of "dead zones" compared to single-intake designs.

### Coway Airmega 400S Filtration

The Coway also uses a dual-intake design, pulling air from both sides through its Max2 Green True HEPA filters. It adds a 4th stage: an optional ionizer (which can be turned off if you prefer no ions). The Coway's pre-filters are washable, which reduces ongoing costs slightly and keeps the primary HEPA filter cleaner for longer. Based on our research, the Coway's filter design is widely regarded as one of the most well-engineered in the consumer purifier market.

**The practical difference** comes down to filter lifespan and cost. Medify recommends replacing filters every 3 to 4 months, which keeps filtration performance optimal but means more frequent replacements. Coway rates its HEPA filters for 12 months, which is more typical and reduces the hassle factor.

## 3. Smart Features

**Winner: Coway Airmega 400S — decisively**

This is where the $150 price gap between these two purifiers becomes easiest to justify.

### Coway Airmega 400S Smart Ecosystem

The Coway Airmega 400S connects to Wi-Fi and integrates with the IoCare app, offering:
- **Real-time air quality monitoring** with a particle sensor and color-coded LED ring
- **Auto mode** that adjusts fan speed based on detected air quality
- **Scheduling and timers** through the app
- **Amazon Alexa and Google Assistant** voice control
- **Filter life tracking** with replacement reminders
- **Remote control** from anywhere via the app
- **Eco mode** that shuts off the fan when air quality is consistently clean, saving energy

Customer reviews frequently highlight the auto mode as one of the Airmega 400S's best features. According to user reports, the sensor is responsive and the fan adjusts smoothly between speeds without jarring transitions.

### Medify MA-40 Controls

The Medify MA-40 is a manual purifier with basic onboard controls: fan speed selection, a timer (up to 8 hours), sleep mode, child lock, and a basic LED air quality indicator light. There is no app, no Wi-Fi, no voice assistant support, and no remote control.

This is not necessarily a negative — some buyers prefer the simplicity of a purifier that just runs without needing an app. But if smart home integration, scheduling, and real-time data are part of your decision criteria, the Coway is the only option here.

## 4. Noise Levels

**Winner: Coway Airmega 400S**

| Setting | Medify MA-40 | Coway Airmega 400S |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Low** | ~25 dB | ~22 dB |
| **Medium** | ~42 dB | ~38 dB |
| **High** | ~56 dB | ~52 dB |

The Coway Airmega 400S is consistently quieter across all fan speeds. At 22 dB on low, it is essentially inaudible in a quiet room — you would need to put your ear near the unit to hear it running. The Medify MA-40 at 25 dB on low is still very quiet and perfectly suitable for bedroom use, but the Coway has a slight edge.

The difference is more noticeable on high speed. The Medify hits approximately 56 dB, which is comparable to a normal conversation. The Coway stays around 52 dB, which is still audible but less intrusive. For daytime use in a living room or office, both are acceptable. For overnight use, both work well on their lowest settings.

According to customer reviews, the Coway's noise quality is also notable — users describe it as a smooth white noise rather than a harsh mechanical sound. The Medify's airflow noise is slightly more noticeable due to its dual-intake design creating airflow from two directions.

## 5. Filter Costs and Long-Term Value

**Winner: Medify MA-40 (annual cost) / Coway Airmega 400S (convenience)**

This section requires careful analysis because the headline filter prices do not tell the whole story.

### Medify MA-40 Filter Economics

Medify recommends filter replacement every 3-4 months. Replacement filters cost approximately $30-40 per set. At a 3-month replacement cycle, that puts annual filter costs at roughly $120-160. However, many owners report stretching filters to 4-5 months in homes with moderate air quality, bringing the effective annual cost closer to $80-120.

Medify also offers a lifetime warranty when you register the product, which is an unusually strong commitment and offsets some of the higher filter frequency.

### Coway Airmega 400S Filter Economics

Coway rates its Max2 filters for 12 months. A replacement set costs approximately $80-100. The washable pre-filter extends the HEPA filter's lifespan by catching large particles first, and customer reviews broadly confirm that the 12-month lifespan is realistic.

### 5-Year Total Cost Comparison

| Cost Factor | Medify MA-40 | Coway Airmega 400S |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Purchase price | $349.99 | $499.99 |
| Filters (5 years) | ~$400-600 | ~$400-500 |
| Electricity (5 years, est.) | ~$60-80 | ~$55-70 |
| **5-Year Total** | **~$810-1,030** | **~$955-1,070** |

The total cost of ownership over five years is surprisingly close. The Medify's lower purchase price is partially offset by more frequent filter replacements. The Coway's higher upfront cost is offset by less frequent, though similarly priced, filter changes.

**The real difference is convenience.** Replacing filters once a year (Coway) versus every 3-4 months (Medify) is a meaningful quality-of-life difference for many owners. Coway also provides app-based filter life tracking, so you never have to guess when a replacement is needed.

## 6. Build Quality and Design

**Winner: Coway Airmega 400S**

The Coway Airmega 400S has a more refined, furniture-grade build. Its rounded square design with mesh side panels and a clean top surface looks intentional in a living room. The LED air quality ring on top provides visual feedback without being garish, and the overall construction feels substantial at 24.7 lbs. Coway has been building air purifiers for over two decades, and the Airmega 400S reflects that experience in its fit and finish.

The Medify MA-40 is a solidly built unit but has a more utilitarian aesthetic. Its rectangular form factor with dual intake grilles on each side is functional rather than decorative. At 20 lbs, it feels lighter and slightly less premium in hand. That said, the build quality is more than adequate for a $350 purifier — it simply does not match the Coway's polish.

Both purifiers have a relatively compact footprint for their CADR output, though neither is what you would call small. The Coway's square shape takes up more floor space but presents a more finished look. The Medify's rectangular profile is narrower but taller.

## Who Should Buy Which

### Buy the Medify MA-40 ($350) if:
- Your room is under 550 sq ft and you want strong H13 HEPA filtration without overspending
- You do not need smart features, app control, or voice assistant integration
- You appreciate Medify's lifetime warranty and are comfortable with more frequent filter replacements
- Budget is a primary consideration and you want to keep the initial purchase under $400
- You prefer a straightforward purifier that you set and forget with manual controls

### Buy the Coway Airmega 400S ($500) if:
- You need to cover a large room or open floor plan exceeding 550 sq ft
- Smart features matter: app control, auto mode, Alexa/Google Assistant, and real-time air quality monitoring
- You prefer less frequent filter changes (once a year versus every 3-4 months)
- Build quality and design aesthetics are part of your purchase criteria
- You want the highest CADR available in the mid-premium category — the 400+ CFM rating is exceptional for under $500
- You value Coway's long-standing reputation and proven track record in air purification

## The Bottom Line

**The Coway Airmega 400S is the better purifier.** It delivers higher CADR, larger room coverage, smarter features, quieter operation, and better build quality. If you can afford the $500 price point, it is one of the most capable and well-rounded air purifiers available at any price.

**The Medify MA-40 is the better value.** At $150 less, it delivers genuine H13 HEPA filtration with a dual-intake design that handles rooms up to 840 sq ft (at 2 ACH) effectively. Its lifetime warranty is a meaningful differentiator, and for buyers whose rooms are under 550 sq ft, it cleans air just as thoroughly as the Coway — it just does it without the smart features and with more frequent filter changes.

Based on our research and analysis of verified customer reviews, the deciding factors come down to room size and smart feature needs. If your space demands 400+ CADR or you want app control, the Coway Airmega 400S is worth the premium. If your space is modest and you prefer simplicity, the Medify MA-40 is an excellent purifier that will not leave you wanting for clean air.
