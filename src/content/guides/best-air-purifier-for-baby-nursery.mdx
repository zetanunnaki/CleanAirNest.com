Your baby breathes faster than you do — about 40-60 breaths per minute compared to an adult's 12-20. That means they inhale more airborne particles relative to their body weight. Choosing the right air purifier for a nursery is one of the most impactful steps you can take for your newborn's respiratory health.

But nurseries have specific requirements that other rooms don't: ultra-quiet operation, zero ozone emissions, and no small parts or cord hazards. Here's everything you need to know.

## What to Look For (and Avoid)

### Must-Have Features

1. **True HEPA (H13) filtration** — Captures 99.97% of allergens, dust, and particles
2. **Ultra-quiet operation** — Under 30 dB on the lowest setting (library quiet)
3. **No ozone emissions** — Look for CARB-certified or ozone-free models
4. **Night light with dimming** — Gentle ambient light without disrupting sleep
5. **Auto mode** — Adjusts fan speed so you don't have to

### Features to Avoid

- **Ionizers without HEPA** — Can produce ozone, a lung irritant
- **UV-C light purifiers** — Can produce ozone and don't add meaningful filtration
- **Ozone generators** — Dangerous for babies (and adults)
- **Essential oil diffuser combos** — Fragrance oils can irritate infant lungs

## Where to Place the Purifier

- **3-6 feet from the crib** — Close enough to clean the breathing zone, far enough to prevent cord hazards
- **On the floor or a sturdy shelf** — Never where a toddler can pull it down
- **Away from windows** — Windows introduce new particles; place the purifier between the window and crib
- **Keep intake vents unobstructed** — Leave at least 12 inches of clearance on all sides
